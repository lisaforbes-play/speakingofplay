Playful pedagogy + play therapy

Play changes everything. We just forgot how to let it.

Most systems we work in were built for control, efficiency, and predictability. Somewhere along the way, curiosity, connection, and humanity got squeezed out.

Play isn’t extra. It isn’t just an icebreaker or a strategy. It’s what brings people back online.

For Colleges & Organizations For Therapists

Same word. Different work.

In educational settings, I focus on how play rehumanizes learning and invites curiosity, risk-taking, and connection back into the room.

In clinical settings, I focus on play as the primary language through which children express, process, and make sense of their internal worlds.

The throughline is play. The application is distinct.

About Lisa

Lisa Forbes is a faculty member at the University of Colorado Denver, a Licensed Professional Counselor (LPC), and a Registered Play Therapist Supervisor (RPT-S™). She co-founded Professors at Play, a global network of faculty exploring playful pedagogy in higher education.

Her work lives at the intersection of teaching, therapy, and human development, bringing a rare blend of research, lived experience, and embodied practice into every space she enters.
