For therapists and mental health professionals

Children don’t always have words. But they are always telling a story.

In play therapy, play is not a technique layered onto treatment. It is the child’s primary language. Through play, children express what they cannot yet say directly, work through internal conflicts, and begin to make sense of their worlds.

My trainings and presentations help clinicians deepen their ability to see what is unfolding in the playroom, recognize the meaning carried in symbols and sequences, and respond in ways that support the child’s process without rushing, directing, or overinterpreting it.

What I offer

My play therapy speaking and training work is grounded in child-centered practice, with a narrative-informed lens that helps clinicians notice what is emerging in play and stay with it more skillfully.

Clinical trainings and conference presentations
Workshops for therapists, supervisors, and mental health professionals
Consultation and supervision support
Case conceptualization through a narrative-informed child-centered lens

What makes this different

This is not about steering play or forcing meaning onto it.

It is about refining attention so clinicians can notice tensions, repetitions, roles, and shifts in the child’s play without taking over the process.

The story doesn’t change because we change it. It changes because it has finally been fully seen and held.

What clinicians leave with

More confidence in non-directive work with children
A clearer sense of what to look for in play sequences
Language that supports the child’s process without interpreting for them
A deeper trust in the therapeutic pace of play therapy
