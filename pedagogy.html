For colleges and organizations

Learning doesn’t need more control. It needs more humanity.

Most classrooms and institutions are built around predictability, performance, and getting it right. But real learning happens when people can explore, risk, question, and not know.

I work with faculty, leaders, and organizations to rethink learning environments through the lens of play. Not as a set of tricks or engagement hacks, but as a deeper shift in how we design, facilitate, and experience learning.

What I offer

My talks and workshops help people move from rigid systems toward curiosity, connection, and presence.

Keynotes that challenge how we think about teaching and learning
Workshops that are immersive, experiential, and human
Faculty development rooted in curiosity, flexibility, and meaning
Conversations that make space for risk-taking and emergent learning

Play is Not Extra

What makes this different

I’m not just offering activities to make learning more fun. I’m challenging the underlying assumptions that make learning feel lifeless in the first place.

This work blends research, lived experience, facilitation, and embodiment. People don’t just hear about play. They experience what shifts when play is allowed back into the room.

What people leave with

A different way of seeing classrooms and learning spaces
Language for what hasn’t been working
Permission to loosen rigid structures
Practical ways to bring play back into learning
